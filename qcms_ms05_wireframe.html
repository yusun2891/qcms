Reading Explorer 3 · Unit 5 · Lesson 1
The Art of Storytelling 검수중
원본 지문
문단 1

Storytelling is one of the oldest forms of human communication, dating back thousands of years to when our ancestors gathered around fires to share tales of the hunt. These narratives served not only as entertainment but also as a means of preserving history, passing down cultural values, and teaching important life lessons to younger generations.

문단 2

Today, despite the rise of digital media and instant communication, the fundamental human need for stories remains unchanged. From podcasts to social media, we continue to share our experiences through narrative structures that would be recognizable to our distant ancestors.

문단 3

The power of storytelling lies in its ability to create emotional connections. When we hear a well-told story, our brains release oxytocin, the hormone associated with empathy and social bonding. This biological response explains why stories can change minds and inspire action in ways that facts and statistics alone cannot.

문단 4

Modern researchers have found that narrative thinking is fundamentally different from logical thinking. While logical thinking helps us analyze and solve problems, narrative thinking helps us understand ourselves and others, make sense of our experiences, and imagine possible futures.
